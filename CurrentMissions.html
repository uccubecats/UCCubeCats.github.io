Current CATiSE Missions

Project Tuxedo

Project Tuxedo is UC CubeCats’ CATiSE mission for the 2021-2022 academic year, and represents our “return-to-flight” after the disruptions caused by COVID-19. The mission will seek to measure air pollutants during its flight, while simultaneously trying to protect an egg from damage in a unique spinoff of the egg drop challenge.

Objectives

  1. Educate first-year or inexperienced members about project management and working as a team, while applying this to a technical project.
  2. Measure air pollution levels at different altitudes.
  3. Contain an egg without it sustaining damage during the flight.
  4. Record a 360° timelapse for the duration of the mission.

High-Altitude Balloon (HAB) System

Communications

The HAB will communicate GPS and sensor data to the ground using the APRS frequency (144.39 MHz). The primary transmitter is a HX1 VHF transmitter connected to the main computer via a Habduino shield. In addition to a primary GPS receiver, a Spot Gen4 GPS is used to track the payload during flight and facilitate payload recovery.

Tuxedo Communication Subsystem block diagram

Flight Systems

The mission will use a 1.5 kg weather balloon connected to the payload container (a styrofoam cooler) by a nylon parachute cord. A 5 ft parachute will allow the payload to descend safely at the end of the mission. The egg will be contained within the styrofoam cooler, with the payload subsystems, and secured using an arrangement of packaging peanuts.

Tuxedo Flight Systems Subsystem block diagram

Software & Payload

The primary control computer for the system is an Arduino Mega 2560, which will log data from the sensors, command the camera to take pictures, and store data. The main sensor suite consists of a CO2 sensor, a barometer, and an Air Quality Index (AQI) sensor capable of measuring CO, NO2, C2H5OH (ethyl alcohol), and volatile organic compound (VOC) concentrations in the surrounding air. Measurements will be taken every 60 seconds (0.01667 Hz). In addition to these sensors two cameras will be carried on board. A GoPro Max will be used to record a 360 video of the entire flight, starting at launch. An ArduCam connected to the main Arduino computer will film the egg while the system is climbing or falling. Data from the cameras and sensors will be collected onto two SD cards (one for the ArduCam and main sensors, the other for the GoPro Max). Power is provided by two 30000 mAh power banks (one powers the Arduino main sensors and ArduCam, the other powers the GoPro Max).

Tuxedo Software & Payload Subsystem block diagram

Mission

The mission will be launched from a location in southern Indiana or northern Kentucky (exact location TBD); This increases the likelihood of the payload landing somewhere close to the Greater Cincinnati area at mission’s end. It is expected that the flight will last around 9 hours and will reach a maximum altitude of 30,000 meters (~100,000 feet). The currently expected launch date is April 23rd, 2022.
